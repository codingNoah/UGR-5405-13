Fresh From Ethiopian Community

Genfo
Genfo, Ga'at,or Marca is a stiff porridge-like substance that is typically shaped into a round shape with a hole in the middle of the dipping sause, a mixture of butter and red peppers, or pulses like sunflower, seed, nut, and flax. Barley or wheat flour is used to make gene.

Doro Wot
Wat, Wot, and Tsebhi are Ethiopian and Ertriean stews made with chicken, beef, lamb, a variety of vegetables, spice blends including berbere, and niter kibbeh, seasoned clarified butter. Doro Wot is a must-have at any celebration including Chirstmas and Easter, thanks to delicate meat, peepery eggs and a creamy, savory sause.

Beyaynetu
This is Ethiopian's most popular vegetarian dish which translates as "a bit of every type," hence your injera will be buried in mounds of delecatable and vibrant veggies, potatoes, curries, lentil soups, and more, creating a riot of hues and tastes.

Kitfo
Kitfo is a typical Ethiopian meal made with raw beef that has been fleshy ground, Ethiopian butter ,and spices like pepper and salt. The dish served with a varieties of bread, with injera being a must-have at every kitfo eatery.
